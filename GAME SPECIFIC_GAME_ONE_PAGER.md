# Game One-Pager

## Working title
The Last Interview

## High-level pitch
A short-form mystery investigation game set in a single location. The player interviews a handful of suspects, inspects evidence, spots contradictions, and makes a final accusation before time runs out.

## Why this version
This is the smallest version that preserves the strongest parts of the broader concept:
- social deduction,
- memory and consequence,
- branching conversation,
- evidence-based reasoning,
- replayability through different outcomes.

It avoids the risky parts of the larger concept:
- open city simulation,
- large NPC populations,
- procedural case generation,
- multimodal production complexity,
- heavy live-AI dependencies,
- creator sandbox tooling.

## Player fantasy
“I am a sharp investigator piecing together truth from people, clues, and contradictions.”

## Core loop
1. Move through the location.
2. Talk to a suspect.
3. Unlock or reveal statements.
4. Find or inspect evidence.
5. Cross-reference statements against evidence.
6. Mark contradictions or grow suspicion.
7. Decide who to accuse.
8. See an ending based on the investigation quality and final choice.

## Smallest fun version
- One location: a hotel lobby / office suite / manor foyer and side rooms.
- One case.
- 3 suspects plus 1 victim/off-screen subject.
- 8–15 evidence items total.
- Branching dialogue with simple state tracking.
- One evidence board or notebook.
- Final accusation screen.
- 2–3 endings.

## Session target
10–20 minutes per run.

## Mood / presentation
- Text-first
- Stylized 2D
- Clear UI
- Strong readability over visual ambition
- Ambient tension rather than horror

## Non-goals for v1
- voice acting
- open world
- procedural generation
- online features
- deep inventory system
- full AI-agent backends
- complex combat or action mechanics
